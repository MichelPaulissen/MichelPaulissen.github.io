The Walk GGJ13

The walk is the game I've made during the Global Game Jam 2013, location Breda, The Netherlands.

The game is about two red pandas who are so close together yet so lonely. As they are walking along an endless road they have to find a way to find eachother.

The game is controlled with two gamepads and requires a Microsoft Kinect to function. The game starts off very gray and bland, subtle clues hint the playes as to what is required to reach the goal of the game.
Eventually the players will find out that if they hold hands (this is registered by the Kinect) the characters start to fly, the colors become vivid, vegetation starts to appear and the world becomes a happier place.

The Walk was a project which was slightly out of our comfort zone on purpose. As all our previous gamejam games were very forused on being a video game in the more traditional sense, we felt like doing something entirely different for a change. Because of this the game didn't have a chance of winning any prizes at the jam. However, the project got an honorable mention from the jury, which was obviously an honor.

Design: Alex Camilleri
Art: Baldi Konijn, Daniel van Leeuwen, Nils Ruisch
Programming: Mark Scheurwater, Michel Paulissen
Music: Alec Holowka, Jack Manhorn, Nathaniel Chambers

Space Tramp Brains Eden Games Jam 2012

Space Tramp is the winning game of the Brains Eden Games Jam 2012 at Cambridge.

The game features a character jumping on a trampoline. However, it's not a normal trampoline, but one with which you can reach space! Jump higher and higher, until you are so far into outer space that Earth is but a tiny dot on your screen.

Control the character using the analog stick on your gamepad or the arrow keys on the keyboard. The game becomes more difficult as you get higher into space, as wind (and solar wind) gets stronger. Get higher to earn a new high score.

This was the first time we as a team used Unity3D. We started off a bit slow because we were not used to the art pipeline that you have to invent when you're without the Unity Asset Server. On top of that, we had to think of ways of solving inevitable scaling issues and figure out how to use some of the more obscure features of Unity. As I at the time was the most experienced with the software I was also busy teaching the software to everybody else.

Design: Alex Camilleri
Programming: Mark Scheurwater, Michel Paulissen
Art: Nils Ruisch, Robert van Duursen
Music: Alex Baldwin, Rob Heslop, Ben Hauke

Ferine Technical project with Nils Ruisch

Ferine is a project I did with Nils Ruisch over the summer holiday of 2011.

The goal was to create a character that could ‘morph’ as realistically as possible, without covering up the process with particles or inbetween ‘blobs’ ( e.g. like in Zelda Twilight Princess ). We figured that an effect like the vanilla World of Warcraft trailer would be doable in real time.

This was the first time I got really in depth with GLSL and its peculiarities.
I had to figure out ways to circumvent the maximum vertex attribute space of my video card at that time,
as essentially it needed all the vertex data of both the morph states and morph between them.
I ended up having to encode the vertex animation weights into a seperate texture, for which I had to write a seperate tool.
All in all it was a valuable learning experience. Besides a programming challenge it was also a technical modeling achievement by Nils, also see his post on the subject.

There Will Be Mountains Brains Eden Games Jam 2013

"There Will Be Mountains" is the winning game of the Brains Eden Games Jam 2013 at Anglia Ruskin University, Cambridge.

The game features the player character, a "Sun God" who has to revive a dead planet. This has to be done while avoiding mountains. There are fire pickups that give you a higher score and combo value. Time your pickups carefully to get the most out of your score.

Currently we can not supply a build as the game was created using the trial version of Unity3D. However, we are aiming to make a public build in late 2013.

This was the second time we as a team have used Unity3D at a game jam. We achieved a very optimal workflow by having the non-programmers also use Mercurial for synchronization, combined with a local LAN setup. Overall, I am very proud of what we were able to do in 48 hours.

Sophie in the Sky Global Game Jam 2012

"Sophie in the Sky" is a platforming game that won second place at the Global Game Jam 2012, location Breda.

It is a simple platform game. You run around and jump between platforms in order to make it to the end. In the meanwhile you can pick up objects that increase your score.

The game was made using my "dex" middleware, and the art and world editing pipeline we used was made by Mark. Because the game was created using primarily (unstable) custom tools and middleware we may have not been as productive as we could have been. Nonetheless, this was a fun and valueable learning experience.

The game turned out to be quite fun to play. This is perhaps the most complex (3D) game that has been conceived using Dex to date, and I am proud that it worked as well as it did during a gamejam.
